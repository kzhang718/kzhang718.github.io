Black holes

Primordial

A primordial black hole is a hypothetical type of black hole that formed not by the gravitational collapse of a large star but by the extreme density of matter present during the universe's early expansion.

Stellar

A stellar black hole (or stellar-mass black hole) is a black hole formed by the gravitational collapse of a massive star. They have masses ranging from about 5 to several tens of solar masses.

Supermassive

A supermassive black hole is the largest type of black hole, on the order of hundreds of thousands to billions of solar masses, and is found in the center of almost all massive galaxies.
